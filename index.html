OVERVIEW

ZhenFund is a Beijing-based seed fund founded by New Oriental co-founders Bob Xu and Victor Wang. ZhenFund was established in 2011 in collaboration with Sequoia Capital China, with the aim of promoting innovation among youth in China. After empowering Chinese students to study abroad, Bob and Victor are now dedicated to helping returnees pursue entrepreneurship in China.

At ZhenFund, we believe that a truly great business starts with an exceptional founder. We are dedicated to helping our entrepreneurs build successful companies by providing them with mentorship and the best resources available.

Bob Xu

Founder

Bob Xu is the founder of ZhenFund and one of China's most renowned angel investors. His success earned him numerous awards from various institutions. These include "2010's most respected angelinvestor" by Founder magazine, "2011 angel investor of the year” by Entrepreneur Magazine, "Best angel investor of 2012" and consecutively in 2013, Zhenfund, the angel investment vehicle that Mr. Xu Xiaoping founded in 2012, was honored as "China's Best Angel Investment Fund" in 2014 and then 2015.


Bob Xu Xiaoping has held the position of Chairman of China Angel Investment Group, Honorary Chairman of China Youth Angel Investment Group, Chairman of the Angel Investment Expert Committee of China Securities Regulatory Commission. He is also a general secretary of Committee 2005, a group of nationally famous people who all have overseas education backgrounds.


Before founding Zhenfund, Bob Xu Xiaoping co-founded China's largest private English training and test coaching group, New Oriental Education (NYSE: EDU) along with Yu Minhong and Wang Qiang. Together they are regarded as “The Three Horsemen” who helped to drive China’s education toward a new global era.


Addressing topics such as studying abroad, education, career guidance, startups, his presentations, interviews, and writings have had an immense impact on China’s future leaders, particularly those studying abroad. Because of his work at New Oriental, he was recognized by Southern People Weekly as 2006's top 50 most charismatic people, and most notably distinguished in 2004 in Forbes Magazine's "China's top 100 Celebrity List." He also made his debut in 2016 in Forbes Magazine's "Midas List" as top venture capital investors in the world.

Victor Wang

Co-Founder

Victor is the Co-Founder of ZhenFund. In addition to being a respected business man, he is also a well-known humanities and philosophy scholar. As such, Victor frequently advises portfolio companies on their mission and vision. He is also a famous collector and lover of rare books.


Prior to ZhenFund, Victor was a Co-Founder of New Oriental Education & Technology Group (NYSE: EDU), the largest provider of private education in China. He served as Vice President and Chairman of the Board of Directors at New Oriental and established New Oriental's spoken English division. His popular English classes were reported in-depth by Time Magazine in 2000. Victor also hosted a popular TV show called "From Word to the World", which was widely broadcasted on more than 30 channels in China.


Victor was honored with Foundation Fellow of Harris Manchester College, University of Oxford in 2015.

Anna Fang

Partner & CEO

Anna Fang is the CEO and Partner of ZhenFund, an angel investment fund active in China's TMT sector. Anna oversees the fund's investments, portfolio management and operations. Since starting the fund in 2011 with Xu Xiaoping, Anna has managed the fund’s over 300 early stage investments. She also sits on the Boards of many of ZhenFund’s investments.


Before joining ZhenFund, Anna worked at GE China in Corporate Business Development, responsible for GE’s M&A and BD efforts in China. Anna started her career as an investment banker with J.P. Morgan in the New York office covering consumer and retail companies.


Anna is an Advisory Board member of Columbia University's East Asia Center and also serves on the Board of Visitors of Columbia College. Anna is also the co-head of Stanford Graduate School of Business’ Beijing Chapter.


Anna received her undergraduate degree from Columbia University, and a Master of Business Administration from Stanford Graduate School of Business.
